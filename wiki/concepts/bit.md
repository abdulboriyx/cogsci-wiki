# Bit (Binary Digit)

## Definition
A bit is the fundamental unit of information in information theory. It represents the amount of information required to decide between two equally likely alternatives.

## Mathematical Representation
The number of bits of information is equal to the base-2 logarithm ($log_2$) of the number of equally likely alternatives.
- 2 alternatives = 1 bit
- 4 alternatives = 2 bits
- 8 alternatives = 3 bits

## Interdisciplinary Uses
- **Computer Science:** The smallest unit of data in a computer.
- **Cognitive Psychology:** Used by researchers like Miller to quantify the amount of information transmitted in experiments on absolute judgment and memory.

# Channel Capacity

## Definition
In cognitive psychology, channel capacity refers to the upper limit on the amount of information an observer can accurately transmit or process about a stimulus. It is often measured in [bits](/wiki/concepts/bit.md).

## Distinctions
- **Span of Absolute Judgment:** This is the channel capacity for making absolute judgments of unidimensional stimuli, which Miller identified as being approximately 2.6 bits (around 6.5 categories).
- **Unidimensional vs. Multidimensional:** Channel capacity increases as more stimulus dimensions are added (e.g., judging both pitch and loudness simultaneously), but the capacity per dimension decreases.

## Interdisciplinary Uses
- **Information Theory:** The term originates from Claude Shannon's work on communication systems, where it defines the maximum rate at which information can be reliably transmitted over a communication channel.

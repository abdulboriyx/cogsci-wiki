# Chunking

## Definition
Chunking is the cognitive process of grouping individual pieces of information into larger, more meaningful units called "chunks." By organizing data in this way, individuals can increase the effective capacity of their [immediate memory](/wiki/concepts/immediate-memory.md).

## Distinctions
- **Bits vs. Chunks:** While a "bit" is a measure of information in a technical sense (the amount of information needed to decide between two equally likely alternatives), a "chunk" is a psychological unit whose size depends on the individual's prior knowledge and the organization of the material.
- **Recoding:** The process of creating chunks is often referred to as [recoding](/wiki/concepts/recoding.md).

## Interdisciplinary Uses
- **Computer Science:** Data compression techniques are analogous to psychological chunking.
- **Linguistics:** Grouping phonemes into words and words into phrases is a form of chunking.

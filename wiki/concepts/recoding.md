# Recoding

## Definition
Recoding is the cognitive process of grouping or organizing information into more complex units ([chunks](/wiki/concepts/chunking.md)) to increase the amount of information that can be held in immediate memory.

## Distinctions
- **Input vs. Output Chunks:** Recoding involves taking input chunks (with fewer bits per chunk) and transforming them into output chunks (with more bits per chunk).
- **Efficiency:** The more bits of information each chunk contains, the more total information a person can remember, given that the number of chunks remains relatively constant (7 ± 2).

## Interdisciplinary Uses
- **Computer Science:** Data encoding and compression.
- **Education:** Mnemonic devices are a form of recoding.

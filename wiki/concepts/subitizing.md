# Subitizing

## Definition
Subitizing is the rapid, accurate, and confident judgment of the number of items in a briefly presented collection, without counting.

## Distinctions
- **Subitizing vs. Estimating:** Subitizing occurs for small sets (typically up to 6 or 7 items) with high accuracy. For larger sets, observers must estimate or count, leading to lower accuracy and increased response time.
- **The Magic Number Seven:** Miller identified the discontinuity in reporting accuracy at around seven items as a potential instance of the [span of absolute judgment](/wiki/concepts/channel-capacity.md).

## Interdisciplinary Uses
- **Developmental Psychology:** Studies how infants and young children develop the ability to subitize before learning to count.

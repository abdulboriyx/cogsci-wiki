# George A. Miller

## Institution and Field
- **Institution:** Harvard University (at the time of his 1956 paper)
- **Field:** Cognitive Psychology, Linguistics, Information Theory

## Core Contributions
- **The Magic Number Seven:** Identified the consistent limits of human information processing in absolute judgment (bits) and immediate memory (chunks).
- **Chunking and Recoding:** Proposed that human memory is limited by the number of meaningful units (chunks) rather than the total amount of information in bits.
- **Cognitive Revolution:** A leading figure in the "cognitive revolution," moving psychology away from behaviorism toward a focus on internal mental processes.

## Key Works
- **Miller (1956):** "The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two: Some Limits on our Capacity for Processing Information"
- **Plans and the Structure of Behavior (1960):** With Galanter and Pribram, introduced the TOTE unit as a replacement for the stimulus-response reflex arc.

## Theoretical Position
- **Cognitivist:** Focused on internal representations and mental processes.
- **Information Processing Approach:** Modeled the human mind using concepts from information theory and cybernetics.

## Controversies
- **Relevance of "Seven":** Miller himself questioned whether the number seven was a fundamental constant or merely a "persecuting integer."
